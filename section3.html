Risk Identification, Verification, and AI Reliability

  • AI models predict likely answers based on training data; they do not "think" or understand information like humans.
  • AI can invent information (hallucinate) or produce responses that sound confident but are false, incomplete, misleading, or low-quality.
  • Teachers must maintain constant human supervision, checking facts, dates, and quotes before using AI content.
  • Be wary of "perfect-sounding" answers with no sources. Always compare AI outputs with trusted websites, books, or official materials.
  • Teach students to view AI as a tool to question, not an absolute truth to trust automatically.
  • Train both students and teachers to detect synthetic content and manipulated multimedia (Deepfakes) through practical workshops.
