Data Protection and Privacy

  • Avoid entering personal, sensitive, or identifiable student information (names, grades, health data, contact details, documents, photos) into AI tools, as many systems store this data to train their models.
  • Apply the principle of data minimization: personal data must only be collected and processed when strictly necessary for the educational goal.
  • Use anonymous, dissociated, or fictional examples when writing prompts so minors cannot be identified.
  • Schools and administrations are solely responsible for data processing and must educate their communities on proper handling.
  • Always check if your school has an AI policy, use approved tools, and verify if parental or school consent is required. If unsure, consult school leadership or a data protection officer.
