Introduction

Teachers already use AI for planning, materials, text adaptation and inspiration. At the same time, they need support in using it critically, safely and professionally.

For example, in Sweden, AI in education is linked to digital competence, source criticism, equality and GDPR. Skolverket has pointed out the importance of school guidelines, while IMY shows why responsible data handling matters.

What our survey shows

  • AI is mainly used for time savings and individualized support
  • Cheating and inaccurate information are the biggest concerns
  • Unclear guidelines – the majority lack clarity
  • Top demand: Practical guidelines + professional development
  • Most teachers talk to students about source criticism and cheating
  • Consensus: AI is both support and challenge – not solely positive or negative

Reflection task

Before using AI this week, choose one teaching activity and ask:

  • Why am I using AI here?
  • What are the benefits?
  • What are the risks?
  • What still requires my own professional judgement?
