Algorithmic Bias and Fairness

  • Algorithms can reflect social, racial, or gender biases due to anomalies in training data or the developers' own biases.
  • Bias affects how different groups are described, whose experiences are included, and what is treated as "normal," which can undermine fairness and inclusion in schools.
  • Teachers should actively look for stereotypes in AI-generated examples, images, and descriptions.
  • Evaluate outputs by asking: Who is missing? Who is portrayed negatively? Try changing the prompt to compare the answers.
  • Perform impact analysis by discussing bias openly with students and analyzing real cases where bias caused discrimination.
  • Promote inclusive design by utilizing AI systems that reflect the diverse perspectives of society.
